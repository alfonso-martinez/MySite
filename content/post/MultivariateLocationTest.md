---
title: Testing the multivariate normal mean vector $\mathbf{\mu}$ when the covariance matrix $\mathbf{\Sigma}$ is known
description: 
---

Let $\mathbf{X}_1, \ldots, \mathbf{X}_n \sim \mathcal_p{N}(\mathbf{\mu}, \mathbf{\Sigma})$ be an independent and identically distributed sample, where $n > p$ and $\mathbf{\Sigma}$ is known. We wish to test the hypothesis:
\begin{align}
H_0: \mathbf{\mu} = \mathbf{\mu}_0 \quad vs. \quad H_1: \mathbf{\mu} \neq \mathbf{\mu}_0
\end{align}
